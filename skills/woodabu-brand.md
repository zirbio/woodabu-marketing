## 1. Brand Identity

### 1.1 Who We Are

**Woodabu** is a sustainable design furniture company that handcrafts exclusive solid wood pieces in Alcobendas, Madrid (Spain). Founded in 2017 by **Francisco "Kiko" Izard Taillefer**, a forest engineer who left corporate life to reconnect with his woodworking roots in Béjar. The name "Woodabu" fuses "wood" + "abuela" (grandmother) — a tribute to his grandmother who reviewed his earliest designs.

**Legal/Brand Name:** Woodabu — Wood Art Studio
**Tagline (ES):** "Cada pieza, un legado de la naturaleza"
**Tagline (EN):** "Each piece, a legacy of nature"

### 1.2 Mission

Create beauty with materials that generate positive impact on the planet and people, through 100% local, sustainable, and handcrafted production. Woodabu aspires to be more than a furniture company — it's a conscious design project.

### 1.3 Vision

A world where conscious consumption drives local artisan economies and protects natural resources.

### 1.4 Core Values

| Value | What it means in practice |
|-------|--------------------------|
| **Artesanal** | Every piece is handmade by professional cabinetmakers, one at a time |
| **Sostenible** | FSC-certified wood, natural dyes, water-based varnishes, ecological packaging |
| **Local** | 100% Spanish production — materials, labor, and shipping |
| **Exclusivo** | No mass production. Each piece is unique due to natural wood variations |
| **Duradero** | Lifetime warranty on every piece. Made to last generations |
| **Impacto positivo** | 1 tree planted per piece sold (via Tree Nation), social programs (UFIL youth employment) |

#### Benefit Hierarchy (Pyramid)

Use this hierarchy to structure messaging. Lead with the hook, build with conviction, close with trust.

| Level | Benefit | Use case |
|-------|---------|----------|
| **Lead benefit (the hook)** | Handcrafted by a forest engineer in Madrid — each piece is unique | First impression: ads, hero banners, subject lines, social captions |
| **Supporting benefit (conviction)** | 100% sustainable: FSC wood, natural finishes, Zero Waste + Pure Oceans collections | Mid-funnel: product pages, carousels, email body, blog intros |
| **Closing benefit (trust)** | Lifetime warranty, 1 tree planted per piece, 300+ Google reviews at 4.9/5 | Decision stage: checkout page, abandoned cart, retargeting, FAQ |

### 1.5 SDG Alignment

- SDG 8: Decent Work and Economic Growth
- SDG 9: Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure
- SDG 12: Responsible Consumption and Production
- SDG 13: Climate Action
- SDG 14: Life Below Water (Pure Oceans collection)

## 2. Brand Essence & Archetype

### 2.1 Brand Essence

**"Craft with Conscience"** — the single irreducible idea behind everything Woodabu does. Every piece, every process, every communication should trace back to this core: the marriage of artisanal mastery and environmental responsibility.

### 2.2 Brand Archetype

- **Primary: Creator** — the maker, the artist, the one who transforms raw material into something lasting and meaningful
- **Secondary: Caregiver** — the protector of forests, oceans, communities, and the next generation

This dual archetype shapes how we speak: with the passion of someone building something beautiful, and the tenderness of someone who knows it matters.

### 2.3 Brand Promise

> "Every Woodabu piece is made by hand, made to last, and made with positive impact."

This is the contract with the customer. Every touchpoint must reinforce it.

### 2.4 Brand Tension / Manifesto

These are the beliefs that give Woodabu its edge — the convictions that separate the brand from pleasant-but-forgettable sustainability messaging:

- **"We believe furniture should outlive the person who buys it."**
- **"We reject the idea that sustainability is a premium feature — it should be the baseline."**
- **"We believe every imperfection in wood grain is evidence of a life lived, not a defect to hide."**
- **"We believe a table should come with a story, not an Allen key."**

Use these in long-form content, campaigns, brand videos, and About page. They are confrontational by design — they draw a line between Woodabu and IKEA/mass-market without naming names.

## 3. Founder Story

**Francisco "Kiko" Izard Taillefer** — Forest Engineer

- Left a multinational engineering position after 7 months, realizing corporate life wasn't his calling
- Returned to Béjar (Salamanca) in early 2017 to reconnect with the region's woodworking heritage
- Started designing furniture and showing prototypes to his grandmother for feedback
- Gradually immersed himself in carpentry, developing what became Woodabu
- Combines traditional artisanal techniques with contemporary design
- Featured in **Outliers Podcast** (Spotify, iVoox) discussing purpose, sustainability, and entrepreneurship
- LinkedIn: Kiko Izard Taillefer

### 3.1 The Béjar Provenance Story

Béjar (Salamanca) is a small town with a deep tradition in woodworking and textiles. Kiko didn't choose it at random — it's where his family roots are, where craft was passed between generations. This provenance anchors the brand in a specific, real place with its own history. When telling the Woodabu story, reference Béjar as the origin point: "Born in Béjar, built in Madrid."

### 3.2 Strategic Narrative Deployment Map

The founder story is the brand's most powerful asset, but different beats resonate in different contexts:

| Context | Lead with | Why |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| **Trust / Credibility** (product pages, FAQ, Google Ads) | Forest engineer background | Technical authority builds confidence at decision stage |
| **Emotional / Brand** (Instagram, campaigns, About page) | Grandmother story ("abuela" naming) | Emotional resonance, memorability, shareability |
| **PR / Media** (press, podcasts, interviews) | Career pivot — left corporate life for purpose | Classic hero-journey hook that journalists love |
| **B2B** (LinkedIn, architect outreach, trade fairs) | Tradition + contemporary design fusion | Speaks to professional credibility and design sophistication |

### 3.3 "Chapter 2" — The Current Story

## 4. Brand Voice & Tone

### 4.1 Core Brand Territory: "Paz, tranquilidad y fuerza"

This phrase — peace, tranquility, and strength — is the emotional territory Woodabu owns. It's not a tagline; it's the feeling a Woodabu piece should evoke in every touchpoint:

- The calm of natural wood in a room
- The tranquility of knowing your purchase was responsible
- The strength of solid, lasting craftsmanship

Every piece of content should ask: "Does this communicate paz, tranquilidad, or fuerza?"

#### Emotional Benefits Ladder

| Level | Statement | Use case |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **Functional** | "Mi mesa está hecha de castaño español con certificación FSC." | Product descriptions, specs, materials pages |
| **Emotional** | "Me siento orgulloso cuando los invitados preguntan por mi mesa y puedo contarles su historia." | Social media, testimonials, email, campaigns |
| **Self-expressive** | "Soy alguien que elige significado por encima de conveniencia." | Brand manifesto, long-form content, About page, brand video |

### 4.2 Voice Characteristics

| Attribute | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Warm** | Like talking to the artisan who made your furniture | "Hemos elegido esta veta de castaño pensando en tu salón" |
| **Authentic** | Real workshop, real hands, real imperfections | "Las marcas naturales de la madera son parte de su historia" |
| **Passionate** | Genuinely enthusiastic about wood and craft | "Cada tabla tiene una vida antes de llegar al taller" |
| **Knowledgeable** | Speaks with authority about materials and processes | "El roble FSC de nuestros proveedores locales garantiza..." |
| **Humble** | Never boastful, lets the work speak | "Intentamos que cada pieza sea un poco mejor que la anterior" |

### 4.3 What We Are NOT

- Corporate or institutional ("Nuestra empresa líder en el sector...")
- Salesy or pushy ("OFERTA! COMPRA YA! SOLO QUEDAN 3!")
- Overly technical or cold ("Producto ref. MDF-2847-B...")
- Greenwashing ("Somos la marca más eco del mundo...")

### 4.4 Tone by Channel

| Channel | Tone | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| **Instagram** | Most casual, visual-first. Storytelling about process, workshop life | Use emojis sparingly. Reels about crafting process perform best |
| **Facebook** | Slightly more informational. Workshop events, delivery updates | Engage with comments warmly |
| **Email** | Personal, like a letter from the workshop. Never templated feeling | 3 subject line variants for A/B testing |
| **Google Ads** | Concise, benefit-focused. Material + value prop in 30 chars | Always mention "madera maciza" or "hecho a mano" |
| **Meta Ads** | Aspirational + emotional. Different angles per audience | Eco segment vs design lovers vs gift buyers |
| **Blog** | Educational, long-form. Expert voice on sustainability and design | SEO-focused but never keyword-stuffed |
| **LinkedIn** | Professional, industry-focused. Impact metrics, founder story | B2B angle: interior designers, architects |
| **WhatsApp** | Warm, responsive, consultative. Like a trusted advisor, not a chatbot | First name, quick replies, voice notes when appropriate |
| **Pinterest** | Inspirational, room-context, keyword-rich descriptions | Think "interior design board" not "product catalog" |
| **TikTok** | Raw, behind-the-scenes, ASMR workshop sounds, educational quick-hits | Less polished, more human. Let the process be the content |

### 4.5 Language

**Primary:** Spanish (Spain market is core)
**Secondary:** English (international shoppers, mainly EU/UK, expats in Spain)

Key difference: English leads with sustainability messaging earlier in the funnel; Spanish emphasizes craftsmanship heritage first.

## 6. Target Personas

### 6.1 Primary Personas

**1. Eco-Conscious Homeowner (30-50)**
- Values sustainability and ethical consumption
- Willing to pay premium for responsible products
- Researches certifications (FSC, local production)
- Motivation: "I want my home to reflect my values"
- Channels: Instagram, blog, email
- Messaging angle: Sustainability, FSC certification, tree planting, Zero Waste collection

**2. Design Enthusiast (28-45)**
- Appreciates unique, artisanal aesthetics over mass-market sameness
- Follows interior design accounts on Instagram
- Values story behind the piece
- Motivation: "I want furniture that's art, not commodity"
- Channels: Instagram, Pinterest, Meta Ads
- Messaging angle: Handcrafted uniqueness, natural wood grain, design story

**3. Quality-Over-Quantity Buyer (35-55)**
- Invests in fewer, better pieces that last generations
- Understands and accepts premium pricing
- Looks for warranties and durability proof
- Motivation: "I'd rather buy once and buy well"
- Channels: Google Ads, email, reviews
- Messaging angle: Lifetime warranty, solid wood durability, made-to-order craftsmanship

**4. Meaningful Gift Shopper (25-50)**
- Looking for special, personal gifts (Christmas, weddings, housewarming)
- Attracted to personalization and custom options
- Values packaging and presentation
- Motivation: "I want a gift that says something"
- Channels: Google Ads (seasonal), Instagram, email campaigns
- Messaging angle: Gift cards, personalizable pieces, "a gift that lasts a lifetime"

**5. Couples Furnishing First Shared Home (28-38)**
- Emotionally driven: building "their" space together for the first time
- Joint decision-making — both partners must agree
- Mix of practical needs (dining table, bed) and aspirational identity
- Budget-conscious but willing to invest in 1-2 "hero pieces"
- Motivation: "We want our first home to feel like us, not like everyone else's"
- Channels: Instagram (discovery), Pinterest (planning boards), Google (research), WhatsApp (questions)
- Messaging angle: "Your first table together should tell your story." Customization, couples choosing together, room bundles

**6. Sustainability-Adjacent Parents (32-45)**
- Actively modeling responsible consumption for their children
- Interested in the "why" behind products — wants to explain choices to kids
- Values educational content about materials, forests, and impact
- Motivation: "I want my kids to grow up understanding that things have a story and a cost to the planet"
- Channels: Instagram, blog, email newsletters
- Messaging angle: "Explain to your kids where their table comes from." Tree planting as a family conversation, workshop visit as an educational experience

### 6.2 Secondary Personas

**7. Interior Designer / Architect (B2B)**
- Looking for unique pieces for client projects
- Needs customization, professional invoicing, reliable delivery
- Channels: LinkedIn, direct contact
- Messaging angle: Custom dimensions, professional treatment, portfolio-worthy pieces

**8. New Home Buyer / Renovator**
- Furnishing a new home or renovating specific rooms
- Shopping by room (bedroom, dining room, terrace)
- Needs guidance on dimensions, combinations, care
- Channels: Blog, Google Ads, FAQ
- Messaging angle: Room-based collections, customization service, 3-5 week delivery

**9. Affluent Empty Nesters (50-65)**
- Children have left home — upgrading to "forever home" quality
- Have budget and patience for artisanal/made-to-order
- Heavy Google searchers, read reviews thoroughly, value phone/WhatsApp consultation
- Less social media native but trust Google reviews and press
- Motivation: "We finally have the space and budget to get the pieces we always wanted"
- Channels: Google Ads (high intent), Google Reviews, blog, email, WhatsApp
- Messaging angle: Lifetime warranty, heirloom quality, "furniture worth waiting for," personalized consultation

**10. Expats in Spain (30-55)**
- English-speaking professionals living in Spain (UK, US, Northern Europe, etc.)
- Want local artisanal products that connect them to their adopted home
- Value the "Made in Spain" story and supporting local economy
- Often furnishing rental or purchased apartments, want meaningful pieces
- Motivation: "I want my home in Spain to feel authentically Spanish, not generic"
- Channels: English-language Instagram, English blog content, Google (English keywords), expat community groups
- Messaging angle: "Made in Madrid by local artisans." English-language service, local craftsmanship, sustainability as a universal language

### 7.5 Pricing Philosophy

Woodabu does not compete on price. The pricing reflects:

1. **Real cost of sustainable materials:** FSC-certified Spanish wood costs more than imported alternatives
2. **True cost of handcraftsmanship:** Every piece is made by skilled artisans, not machines on a production line
3. **Lifetime value:** A Woodabu table at €1,200 over 30+ years = €3.33/month. An IKEA table at €300 over 3 years = €8.33/month
4. **Positive externalities included:** Tree planting, social programs, ocean cleanup — these are built into the price, not bolted on as marketing
5. **No sales, no discounts, no promotions:** The price is honest from day one. No one feels cheated for buying at full price

When addressing price objections, never apologize. Reframe: "You're not paying for a table. You're paying for the forest engineer who selected your wood, the artisan who shaped it, the tree planted in your name, and the guarantee that it will outlast you."

## 22. Sensory Brand Identity

Woodabu is experienced through the senses. This section guides how to evoke the brand's physical identity in digital content.

### 22.1 Auditory Identity

- **Workshop sounds:** The rhythm of sanding, the scrape of a plane on wood, the tap of a mallet, the buzz of a saw. These are brand sounds
- **ASMR potential:** Close-mic recordings of hand-finishing processes (sanding, oiling, waxing) for Reels and TikTok
- **Ambience:** No background music in workshop videos — let the tools and the wood speak. If music is needed, use minimal acoustic guitar or ambient piano
- **Voice:** Kiko's voice in voiceovers and Stories. Calm, unhurried, knowledgeable

**Content applications:**
- Instagram Reels: Open with 2 seconds of sanding sound before any music
- TikTok: "ASMR in the workshop" series — pure process sounds, no talking
- YouTube: Full workshop ambient soundscape for longer videos

### 22.2 Tactile Identity

- **Hand-finished grain texture:** The feeling of running your hand across a Woodabu surface — smooth but alive, with the grain still perceptible under your fingertips
- **Weight:** Solid wood has heft. Show this in video — the effort of lifting, the satisfying thud of placing a piece
- **Temperature:** Wood is warm to the touch, unlike metal or glass. Reference this in copy: "Una superficie que no está fría por las mañanas"

**Content applications:**
- Close-up video: Hands running across finished surfaces, catching the light on grain patterns
- Product photography: Show texture through raking light (low-angle lighting that reveals grain depth)
- Copy cue: "Pasa la mano por la superficie. Eso que sientes es castaño español acabado con aceite natural"

### 22.3 Olfactory Identity

The scent profile of a Woodabu workshop and its products:

- **Pine resin:** Fresh, clean, forest. The scent of raw pino silvestre when it arrives at the workshop
- **Beeswax:** Warm, honey-like. Applied as a natural finish
- **Linseed oil:** Earthy, slightly nutty. Used in natural finishes
- **Sawdust:** The ambient scent of the workshop — wood in transformation

**Content applications:**
- Copy: "Cuando abras la caja, olerás a pino y cera de abeja. Es el olor de la madera recién acabada"
- Video: Reference scent in voiceovers — "Aquí en el taller siempre huele a madera. Es el mejor perfume del mundo"
- Workshop visits: The scent experience is one of the strongest memories visitors take home — reference it in post-visit content
- Future opportunity: Include a small sachet of wood shavings in the packaging as a sensory brand touchpoint

### 22.4 Visual Identity (Reinforcement)

Already covered in Section 11.2, but reinforced here for completeness:

- **Warm lighting:** Golden hour quality, never clinical white
- **Workshop patina:** The lived-in beauty of a working workshop — tools, shavings, works-in-progress
- **Human hands:** Always include hands in process imagery. Hands are the bridge between raw material and finished piece
- **Wood close-ups:** Macro shots of grain, knots, oil absorption, finishing processes
- **In-situ product shots:** Pieces in real homes, not white-background studio shots
